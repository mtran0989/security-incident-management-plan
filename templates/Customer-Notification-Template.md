## Pre-Notification Checklist

Before sending any customer notification:

- [ ] Legal Counsel has confirmed notification obligation is triggered
- [ ] Legal Counsel has approved the notification content and timing
- [ ] CCO has reviewed and approved the communication
- [ ] CISO has approved the technical accuracy of the description
- [ ] Customer list has been validated by the Data Owner
- [ ] Delivery method and logging mechanism confirmed
- [ ] Regulatory notification (if required) has been filed or is simultaneous

## Template B — Cardholder Data Breach Notification (PCI DSS)

> *Use only when CHD compromise is confirmed. Requires immediate coordination with Legal and PCI QSA.*

**SUBJECT:** Urgent Security Notice — Payment Information

---

Dear [Customer Name],

We are contacting you regarding a security incident that may have affected payment card information processed through NexaCore Technologies' systems.

**What Happened**

Between approximately [DATE RANGE], unauthorized individuals may have had access to payment card information processed through our systems. We detected this activity on [DETECTION DATE] and immediately initiated our incident response procedures.

**What Information May Be Affected**

Payment card information that may have been involved includes:
- Card number (PAN)
- Cardholder name
- Expiration date
- [Other applicable data elements]

**Immediate Steps You Should Take**

1. **Contact your card issuer immediately** using the number on the back of your card to report potential compromise and request a new card number
2. Review your recent card statements for unauthorized transactions
3. If you identify unauthorized charges, report them to your card issuer promptly

You are generally not responsible for fraudulent charges when you report them promptly. Your card issuer can provide specific guidance on their zero-liability policies.
